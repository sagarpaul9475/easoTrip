Our Ladakh package offers an unforgettable journey through one of the most stunning landscapes in the world. You'll explore breathtaking valleys, ancient monasteries, and serene lakes while experiencing the unique culture of this remote region.

  • Drive through Valleys with faboulous views.

  • Experience the vibrant local Heritage in Kullu.

What to Bring

  • Warm Clothing: The temperature can drop significantly, especially at night. Layers are recommended.
  • Comfortable Footwear: You'll be doing a fair amount of walking, so sturdy, comfortable shoes are essential.
  • Personal Medication and First Aid Kit: Carry any personal medication you might need, along with basic first aid supplies.
  • Sun Protection: Sunglasses, sunscreen, and a hat are crucial due to the strong UV rays at high altitudes.
  • Camera: Don't miss the opportunity to capture the stunning landscapes and vibrant culture.
  • Valid ID Proof: Ensure you have a valid government ID for travel and permits.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Manali is between June and September, when the weather is pleasant and the roads are open. During these months, you can enjoy clear skies, beautiful landscapes, and the various festivals that take place in the region.

Travel Tips

  • Acclimatization: Manali is at a high altitude, so it’s important to spend the first day acclimatizing to avoid altitude sickness. Drink plenty of water and avoid strenuous activities.
  • Permits: Certain areas in Manali require special permits. We’ll assist you in obtaining these, but ensure you have copies of your ID and passport-sized photos.
  • Connectivity: Mobile connectivity is limited in Manali. BSNL has the best coverage, but it’s advisable to inform family and friends about limited access before you go.
  • Cash: ATMs are sparse, especially in remote areas, so carry enough cash for personal expenses.
  • Local Etiquette: Manali people are known for their hospitality. Respect local customs, dress modestly, and always ask for permission before taking photos of people or their homes.

Health & Safety

Make sure you’re physically prepared for the trip, as the high altitude can be challenging. Consult with your doctor, especially if you have any pre-existing conditions. Travel insurance is highly recommended to cover any medical emergencies.
